Alex Lopez

I’m a master’s student in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at EPFL and a visiting researcher in the Responsive Environments group at the MIT Media Lab, working on human augmentation and assistive technologies.

My interests lie in the intersection of ultra-low-power wearables and embedded AI, with the goal of building self-contained assistive devices that restore and extend human capabilities, especially for people who have limited access to specialised care and equipment.

I am particularly drawn to systems that sit quietly on the body, sense subtle physiological or mechanical signals, and turn them into something useful in daily life, whether that is helping someone control a prosthetic limb, manage their glucose levels more easily, or communicate when speech is no longer possible.
