Living Knitwork Pavilion

Interactive textile architecture with capacitive sensing | 2023

About

The Living Knitwork Pavilion is an 18-foot tall dodecagonal structure at Burning Man 2023 that uses capacitive sensing to create an interactive lighting and audio experience. Conductive yarns knitted into the fabric act as antennas, detecting visitor movement through changes in electrical field coupling.

Electronics & Interaction

Conductive yarn integrated into the 12 fabric petals connects to analog circuitry that senses electrical field changes as people move nearbyâ€”similar to how a theremin works. The detected movement data drives a lighting and audio system in real time, creating a responsive environment that reflects visitor presence and activity.

Technical Implementation

  • Sensing fabric: Conductive yarn knitted into textile structure
  • Analog circuitry: Amplifiers, filters, and converters for signal processing
  • Capacitive sensing: Detects body movement through electrical field coupling
  • Outputs: Spatial audio and lighting response
  • Power: Solar cells and batteries (no generators)

Materials & Construction

Built using 3D-knitting with functional yarns including conductive, photochromic, and luminous fibers on recycled polyester. The textile manufacturing process enabled multi-layer fabrics with integrated channels for electrical cabling.
